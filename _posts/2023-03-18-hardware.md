# Layers

<img src="layers.png" alt="layers">

## Outline layer
- Defines the board's physical shape.

> Many fab shops will not commit to fabricate your board without an outline layer.
An endmill will drill the board out from the rest of the panel (also called routing out). Without the outline layer, the fab shop does not know where your board ends and another customer’s board begins in the large panel in which they are fabricated. 

## Drill layer
- Information about what hole sizes are used for each hole and their precise location.

> Used by a precision CNC drilling machine to drill every hole in the board. 

## Silk Screen
The silk screen is sometimes referred to as the overlay layer on both
the top and bottom of the board. This is the printed text or numbers
that identity each part and any other written reference information.
It can also include images, logos, and pictures.

## Solder Mask
The soldermask is a layer of dielectric with small openings in it to
reveal the underlying metal. Components will be soldered to pads
exposed in the open areas of soldermask. The soldermask coating
prevents the solder from flowing over the rest of the covered copper
traces. These windows expose the pad on to which will be soldered the components.

Protects
If it weren’t for the soldermask, solder would spread across the traces and not enough solder would stay on the pads to make a reliable solder joint for the component. The soldermask can also sometimes prevent solder bridging between closely spaced pads. protects the fine lines of interconnects from mechanical abuse.


If you are selling your boards, to keep a RoHS (Restriction of
Hazardous Substances) certification, it is worth the extra price to
select lead-free HASL and qualify all of your components to be leadfree
as well.



### Grounding plane

Using a ground plane on the bottom layer of a circuit board will reduce some sources of noise. If you can fit all or most of your interconnects on the top layer, using the bottom layer for a continuous ground has a favorable ROI. This is a best design practice. 


For example, an important best design practice is to use a continuous ground plane on the layer adjacent to signal layers. This best design practice will reduce the risk of ground bounce noise, a type of cross talk and a type of switching noise, which is often a source of unexpected product failure.
